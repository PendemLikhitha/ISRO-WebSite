Launch Vehicles
Explore the powerful rockets that carry India's spacecraft into the cosmos

Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV)

The PSLV is India's workhorse rocket, capable of launching a variety of satellites into orbit.

  • Height: 44.5 meters
  • Payload capacity: 1,600 kg to Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO)
  • First launch: September 20, 1997
  • Number of successful launches: 51

Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV)

The GSLV is a more powerful rocket, capable of launching heavier satellites into geostationary orbit (GEO).

  • Height: 49 meters
  • Payload capacity: 2,500 kg to GEO
  • First launch: April 18, 2001
  • Number of successful launches: 14

Small Satellite Launch Vehicle (SSLV)

The SSLV is India's newest rocket, designed to launch small satellites into orbit.

  • Height: 34 meters
  • Payload capacity: 500 kg to LEO
  • First launch: August 7, 2022
  • Number of successful launches: 2
